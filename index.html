Correlations Discovered Between Health and Poverty

The most common source of health insurance in the US is employer-sponsored. Therefore, lack of healthcare coverage are associated with decreased access to health care, unmet medical drug needs and lack of preventive health care resulting in poor health. Since health directly affects the employability we see some strong co-relation between lack of health care and poverty.

States like Massachusetts have lowest lack of healthcare (4.6%) and low poverty rate(11.6%)

Texas has the highest lack of healthcare insurance (24.9%) and poverty level is higher than average among all of the US states
